FAQ

Common questions

What paper do you print on? +
We use 270 GSM fine art matte paper — the same paper used by professional fine art photographers and galleries. It is acid-free, OBA-free, and rated for 400+ years of colour stability under normal display conditions.
How many colours are used in printing? +
We use a 12-channel pigment ink system. This includes standard CMYK, light cyan, light magenta, vivid light magenta, photo black, matte black, light black, light light black, and green/orange channels for extended gamut reproduction. The result is colours more vibrant than sRGB and blacks that are truly neutral.
Will my colours look the same as on screen? +
We use ICC colour profiles and soft proofing to match print output as closely as possible to your screen. For best results, we recommend calibrating your monitor and designing in sRGB. Our 12-colour ink system can reproduce a wider gamut than most screens can even display.
What sizes are available? +
All art prints are 4×6 inches (101×152mm) in portrait orientation, or 6×4 inches (152×101mm) in landscape. You can switch between orientations in the editor. Each print includes 5mm bleed for clean edge-to-edge printing.
How are the prints packed for shipping? +
Prints are packed flat in rigid board-backed mailers that prevent bending in transit. For orders of 12+ prints we use reinforced flat boxes. Each print is interleaved with glassine paper to prevent surface contact.
Can I order fewer than 4 prints? +
Our minimum order is 4 prints at ₹800. Prints come in sets of 4 — you can design each print differently within a set, or use the same design across all prints in a set.
